<p><ul><li>Inductive Conductivity Sensor</li><li>Electrodeless Conductivity Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The water conductivity sensor market is primarily categorized into two types: inductive conductivity sensors and electrodeless conductivity sensors. Inductive conductivity sensors use electromagnetic induction to measure conductivity without direct contact with the liquid, offering advantages in harsh environments. In contrast, electrodeless conductivity sensors utilize capacitive methods, allowing for non-invasive measurements that prevent contamination. Both types serve critical roles in various industries, including water treatment, aquaculture, and environmental monitoring, ensuring accurate assessments of water quality and conductivity levels.</p></p>
