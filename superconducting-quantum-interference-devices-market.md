<p><p>Superconducting Quantum Interference Devices (SQUIDs) are categorized into two main market types: AC and RF. AC SQUIDs are primarily utilized in measuring magnetic fields and sensitive magnetometry applications, benefiting from their high sensitivity to alternating currents. In contrast, RF SQUIDs operate at radio frequencies, enabling faster signal processing and are commonly employed in applications such as quantum computing and high-speed digital circuits.
