<p><p>Dissolving Wood Cellulose (DWC) is a key ingredient in producing regenerated cellulose products, such as viscose and lyocell fibers. Market types for DWC include alkali wood pulp (or sulfate wood pulp), which is produced through the kraft process and is characterized by higher strength and durability. On the other hand, sulfite wood pulp is derived from the sulfite process, allowing for a more delicate fiber with distinct properties. Both types cater to diverse applications in textiles, food, and pharmaceutical industries.</p></p>
